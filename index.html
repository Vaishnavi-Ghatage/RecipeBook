BHINDI MASALA

Ingredients:

  • 250 gms bhindi
  • 1 bowl water
  • small 7-8 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 tsp jeera seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 bowl onion finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • to taste Salt
  • 1 tsp fennel powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp aamchoor powder
  • 1/4 tsp pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp lime juice

Recipe:

  1. Heat oil add jeera and fennel seeds, let them splutter.
  2. Add the onions and lightly brown them.
  3. Add ginger and some water, stir.
  4. Add the turmeric powder and stir again.
  5. Mix in the bhindi. Pour in the rest of the water and continue stirring.
  6. Mix in the bhindi well and add the salt.
  7. Sprinkle fennel powder, amchoor powder and sugar.
  8. Mix again and sprinkle the pepper powder.
  9. Add the lime juice, stir well and serve


CHANA MASALA

Ingredients:

  • 1 Cup hara chana
  • 2 potatoes(boiled)
  • 1 onion
  • finely chopped1 large tomato
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tbsp garlic
  • grated 1/2 ginger
  • grated2 Green chillies
  • to taste Salt
  • red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • for garnishing coriander leaves

Recipe:

  1. Soak hara chana for about one hour, then pressure cook with some salt till soft.
  2. Drain the chana and mash them with a masher or fork. Also lightly mash boiled potatoes and keep aside.
  3. In a pan, heat oil or ghee. Add cumin seeds and wait till they crackle.
  4. Add chopped onion, green chillies and grated garlic. Saute till onion turns brown.
  5. Add tomatoes, ginger, salt and red chilli powder. Cook till tomatoes wilt.
  6. Add mashed hara chana and boiled potatoes. Mix well. Cook for another 6-7 minutes.
  7. Garnsih with coriander leaves and serve


DHOKLA

Ingredients:

  • 250 gms khaman dhokla (yellow)
  • 1/2 cup jaggery
  • grated/ powdered2 lemon-sized tamarind pieces
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 green chillies
  • roughly chopped1 tbsp coriander
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required

Recipe:

  1. Soak the tamarind in a cup of hot water for 10-15 minutes. Then squeeze and extract the pulp. Keep aside.
  2. In a pan or kadhai, heat oil on medium flame and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves and asafoetida.
  3. Add the green chillies and pour in the tamarind pulp. Mix well.
  4. Next, add turmeric and chilli powder. Mix and then add the jaggery and 2 cups of hot water with salt to taste. Stir to combine.
  5. Bring the rassa to a boil and garnish with coriander.
  6. Place the khaman dhoklas inside a bowl of the rassa and enjoy immediately. Alternatively, you can serve the rassa and dhoklas separately.


EGG CURRY

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 Eggs
  • 1 tsp Turmeric
  • 1 tsp Chilli powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
  • 1 tsp Cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp Sesame seeds
  • 1 Mace
  • 2 Cardamoms
  • 2 Onions
  • 1 cup Grated coconut
  • 5-6 tbsp Oil
  • to taste Salt
  • Coriander leaves, for garnishing

Recipe:

  1. First, we need to boil the eggs. To make the masala paste, add 1 tbsp oil to a pan and heat over low flame. Add a cinnamon stick or powder, sesame seeds, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, cloves, mace, and cardamom. Saute till it releases an aroma.
  2. Now add sliced onions, ginger, garlic, and a pinch of salt. Saute it till it changes colour. Add dry grated coconut and stir till it gets a beautiful golden colour.
  3. Turn off the flame and add sesame seeds. Once the masala is cooked. Grind it in a blender and make a thick paste of it.
  4. To prepare anda rassa, heat oil in a pan and add turmeric and red chilli powder. Now add the pre-made paste to the pan.
  5. Mix well and sprinkle salt as per your taste. When the masala releases oil, consider it cooked.
  6. Add the boiled eggs to the masala and mix well. You can also use fried boiled eggs. Pout in some hot water. Make sure it's not too much as the curry should be a little thick.
  7. Cover the lid and let it cook on medium heat for 5-10 minutes. Once cooked just give the egg curry a good mix. You can garnish it with fresh coriander or curry leaves.


PANEER MASALA

Ingredients:

  • 2 Cups Paneer (cubed, deep fried to a light brown)
  • 2 cups Green peas (shelled)
  • 3-4 Green chillies (slit a little)
  • 2 cups Onions, chopped
  • 1 tsp Garlic clove (ground with onions), peeled
  • 1 tsp Ginger, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Tomato, grated
  • 1/4 cup Oil
  • 2 tsp Cumin seeds
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric
  • 1 tbsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp Red pepper, powdered
  • 1 tbsp Coriander seeds, powdered
  • 1 tbsp Coriander leaves (for garnish), chopped

Recipe:

  1. Blend the onions, garlic and ginger to a paste.
  2. Heat oil and add the cumin seeds and the bay leaves. Add onion paste and saute till brown and fat separates.
  3. Add tomatoes, turmeric, salt, garam masala, red pepper and the coriander powder, and stir-fry till fat separates.
  4. Add the peas, paneer and the green chillies and saute over high heat till glossy.
  5. Add two cups water, bring to a boil and simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with the coriander leaves. Omit the chillies if you so desire


POHE

Ingredients:

  • 1 Cup Poha, soaked
  • 1 Bowl Coconut Milk
  • Jaggery, as per taste
  • Tamarind pulp, as per taste
  • 2 Green Chillies (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • A pinch of Asafoetida
  • 1 Sprig Curry Leaves
  • 1 Whole red chilli
  • 1 tbsp Ghee
  • 1 tsp Freshly chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt, as per taste

Recipe:

  1. Soak jaggery and tamarind in water separately and strain the water.
  2. In a bowl of coconut milk, add the jaggery and tamarind water, salt, green chillies, coriander leaves and salt. Mix everything together.
  3. Prepare a tadka. Heat ghee, add red chilli, jeera, mustard seeds, curry leaves and hing and let the spices splutter.
  4. Add the tadka to the coconut milk and pour the milk on soaked poha.
  5. And kolache pohe is ready to be relished.
